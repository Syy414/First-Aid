CPR for an Adult

Chest compression (30 compressions)

Location: Place one hand on the centre of the casualty's chest. Place the heel of your other hand on top of the first and interlock your fingers, keep your fingers off the casualty’s ribs.

Rate: 100 - 120 compressions per minute

Depth: 5 - 6 cm (2-2.5 inches)

Recoil: Allow complete recoil of the chest after each compression

Minimise interruption on chest compression

Rescue breathing (2 times): Open the victim’s airway using head-tilt and chin-lift method. Pinch the nostrils closed, allow their mouth to fall open. Take a breath, seal your lips over the casualty’s mouth, blow steadily until the chest rises. Maintaining the open airway, take your mouth away from the casualty’s. Look along the chest and watch it fall.

CPR for a Child (1 year - puberty)

Give 5 initial rescue breaths before starting CPR

Chest compression (30 compressions)

Location: Place the heel of one hand ONLY on the centre of the chest.

Rate: 100 - 120 compressions per minute

Depth: ⅓ of the child’s chest depth

Recoil: Allow complete recoil of the chest after each compression

Minimise interruption on chest compression

Rescue breathing (2 times): Open the victim’s airway using the head-tilt/chin-lift method. Pinch the nostrils closed, allow their mouth to fall open. Take a breath, seal your lips over the casualty’s mouth, blow steadily until the chest rises. Maintaining the open airway, take your mouth away from the casualty’s. Look along the chest and watch it fall.

CPR for an Infant (< 1 year)

Give 5 initial rescue breaths before starting CPR

Do not use an AED on an infant

Chest compression (30 compressions)

Location: Place the tips of your index and middle finger on the center of the chest.

Rate: 100 - 120 compressions per minute

Depth: ⅓ of the child’s chest depth

Recoil: Allow complete recoil of the chest after each compression

Minimise interruption on chest compression

Rescue breathing (2 times): Open the infant’s airway using head-tilt / chin-lift method. Pinch the nostrils closed, allow their mouth to fall open. Take a breath, seal your lips over the casualty’s mouth, blow steadily until the chest rises. Maintaining the open airway, take your mouth away from the casualty’s. Look along the chest and watch it fall.
