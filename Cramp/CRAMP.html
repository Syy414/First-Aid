Cramp Relief Guide

What is Cramp About?

A sudden painful spasm in one or more muscles, commonly occurring during sleep or after strenuous exercise.

Foot

  • Help the casualty stand with his weight in the front of his foot (or rest the foot on your knee) to stretch the affected muscles.
  • Once the spasm has passed, massage the affected part of the foot with your fingers.

Calf Muscles

  • Help the casualty straighten his knee and support his foot.
  • Flex his foot upwards towards his shin to stretch the calf muscles.
  • Massage the affected area on the back of the calf.

Anterior Thigh

  • Help the casualty lie down.
  • Raise the leg and bend the knee to stretch the muscles.
  • Massage the affected muscles once the spasm has passed.

Posterior Thigh

  • Help the casualty lie down.
  • Raise the leg and straighten the knee to stretch the muscles.
  • Massage the area once the spasm has passed.

Note: Cold spray or analgesic cream can be used to relieve muscle pain. Do NOT use both together as it can cause cell damage due to sudden temperature change.
