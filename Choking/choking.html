Choking Awareness

Foreign body airway obstruction: A life-threatening medical emergency

Recognition

Mild Obstruction

Difficulty in speaking, coughing, and breathing.

Severe Obstruction

Inability to speak, cough, or breathe. Eventually, the casualty will become unresponsive.

Choking Procedures

Adult / Child (1 Year - Puberty)

  1. Encourage the casualty to cough hard.
  2. If it fails, give five back blows.
  3. Bend the casualty forward, give five abdominal thrusts (Heimlich manoeuvre).
  4. Check the mouth for obstructions.
  5. Repeat if choking persists; call 999 if necessary.

Infant (< 1 Year)

  1. Lay the infant facing down along your forearm.
  2. Give 5 back blows between the shoulders.
  3. Turn the infant over, check the mouth for obstructions.
  4. If back blows fail, give 5 chest thrusts.
  5. If choking persists, call 999 and repeat steps until help arrives.
